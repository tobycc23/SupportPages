Privacy Policy for Chordz

Effective date: April 24, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Chordz ("the app") handles information when you use the macOS app.

1. Data Collection

Chordz does not collect personal information from you.

Chordz does not require account creation or login.

2. How the App Works With Input Data

  • MIDI note input and computer keyboard note input are processed locally on your Mac to run chord detection and gameplay.
  • This input data is not sent to external servers by the app.

3. Data Stored Locally on Your Device

Chordz stores app preferences and gameplay data locally (for example, settings and leaderboard entries), including:

  • Preferred MIDI device name and MIDI range settings
  • Chord practice options and play level selection
  • Accidental display preference and keyboard MIDI toggle setting
  • Local leaderboard history for Play mode

This information is stored in local app configuration files such as user-config.json.

4. Data Sharing

Chordz does not sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties.

5. Analytics, Advertising, and Tracking

  • Chordz does not include third-party advertising SDKs.
  • Chordz does not include third-party analytics SDKs.
  • Chordz does not perform cross-app tracking.

6. Internet and Network Use

Chordz is designed to function without sending user data to a backend service controlled by the app developer.

7. Data Retention and Deletion

Data stored by Chordz remains on your device until you remove it (for example, by deleting the app and related app data).

8. Children's Privacy

Chordz is a music education/practice app and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

9. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Updates will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.
